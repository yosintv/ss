Spider Solitaire Scoring Explained — How Points Work

Updated May 17, 2026 · 6-minute read

Spider Solitaire scoring is simple in concept but full of small details that affect how you should actually play the game. Whether you’re trying to grind a high Spider Solitaire score, beat your personal best, or just understand why your moves changed your score by what they did, this Spider Solitaire scoring guide covers the full scoring formula, how XP scoring works alongside the classic score, and the gameplay choices that maximize Spider Solitaire score on every mode.

The Classic Spider Solitaire Scoring Formula

Most online Spider Solitaire games, including spidersolitaire.xyz, use this Spider Solitaire scoring formula:

  • Starting Spider Solitaire score: 500 points.
  • Per move: −1 point. Every single move you make in Spider Solitaire decreases your score by one.
  • Per two seconds of clock time: −1 point. Slow Spider Solitaire games score lower.
  • Per completed suit: +100 points. Up to +800 across all eight Spider Solitaire foundations.
  • On victory: +50 bonus points.
  • Floor: Spider Solitaire score never goes below zero, even in long losing games.

The Spider Solitaire score updates live during play. The win screen captures the final value, and statistics persist your best Spider Solitaire score per difficulty.

What’s a Good Spider Solitaire Score?

Score expectations by Spider Solitaire mode:

  • Spider Solitaire 1 Suit: 700–900 is a strong win. Above 900 is excellent.
  • Spider Solitaire 2 Suits: 600–800 is a strong win. Above 800 is excellent.
  • Spider Solitaire 4 Suits: 500–700 is a strong win. Above 700 is excellent given the difficulty.

The Spider Solitaire scoring formula naturally biases against the harder modes — Spider Solitaire 4 Suits games take more moves and more time, both of which subtract score. A 600 in Spider Solitaire 4 Suits represents more skill than a 750 in Spider Solitaire 1 Suit.

How to Maximize Your Spider Solitaire Score

Three levers control your Spider Solitaire score: number of moves, elapsed time, and completed suits. Push all three in your favor and your Spider Solitaire score climbs.

  • Minimize moves. Use double-click auto-move to skip drag chains. Avoid “adjusting” positions for no reason. Every wasted Spider Solitaire move costs a point.
  • Move steadily, but don’t panic. A 10-minute Spider Solitaire game loses 300 score from time alone (1 point per 2 seconds × 600 seconds). A 4-minute Spider Solitaire game loses 120.
  • Complete every suit. Each completed Spider Solitaire suit is worth 100 points. Eight suits = 800 points. That’s the biggest single Spider Solitaire scoring lever.
  • Win. The +50 victory bonus is small but it’s yours for free if you complete all eight Spider Solitaire foundations.

Spider Solitaire XP vs. Score: Two Separate Systems

On spidersolitaire.xyz, the classic Spider Solitaire score lives alongside a modern XP system. They’re different metrics:

  • Score is per-game. Stats track your best Spider Solitaire score per difficulty.
  • XP is cumulative. Every Spider Solitaire win adds XP toward your account level. XP unlocks themes, card backs, and achievements.

The XP formula rewards different things than the score formula. Score punishes time; XP rewards speed-bonus thresholds (under 5 minutes, under 2 minutes). Score has no “no-undo” bonus; XP gives a +50% multiplier for undo-free Spider Solitaire wins. Score gives the same victory bonus on every difficulty; XP multiplies awards 1×, 1.5×, 2.5× for Spider Solitaire 1, 2, 4 Suits respectively.

Spider Solitaire Scoring and God Mode

God-mode wins on spidersolitaire.xyz don’t award XP and don’t update stats — but the classic Spider Solitaire score still calculates. If you turn on god mode, you’ll see a Spider Solitaire score on the win screen but it won’t affect your best-score record. This separation keeps casual/relaxed play from polluting your stats while still showing you how the game went.

Tracking Your Spider Solitaire Score Over Time

The Stats panel on spidersolitaire.xyz shows your best Spider Solitaire score per difficulty alongside best time, current streak, and win rate. Watching your best Spider Solitaire score climb week over week is a great motivator. Most players see their best Spider Solitaire score climb 50–100 points per month for the first few months of serious play.

Five Spider Solitaire Scoring Mistakes

  • Move-padding. Making “cosmetic” Spider Solitaire moves that don’t change the position costs free points.
  • Slow Spider Solitaire decision-making. A two-minute pause to find the perfect move costs 60 score points.
  • Quitting before completing the last few suits. Each completed Spider Solitaire suit is worth 100 points — even if the position is dire, push for one more before quitting.
  • Excessive undo. Undo doesn’t cost Spider Solitaire score directly, but the moves you undo and redo do. A heavy-undo Spider Solitaire game inflates the move count.
  • Ignoring the win bonus. A barely-won Spider Solitaire game beats a beautifully-played 70% completion — the +50 victory bonus puts you above.
